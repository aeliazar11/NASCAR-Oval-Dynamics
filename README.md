# NASCAR Oval Dynamics Simulator

MATLAB model analyzing the lateral dynamics of a NASCAR Cup Series 
car navigating banked oval corners at superspeedway tracks.

## What This Project Models
- Lateral g-force as a function of speed and banking angle
- Inside vs outside tire load transfer during cornering
- Three-track comparison: Daytona, Charlotte, and Talladega
- Wedge adjustment effect on rear cross weight and handling balance

## Tools
- MATLAB

## Key Results
- Charlotte produces the highest lateral loads despite less banking 
  due to its tighter 213m corner radius
- Talladega generates the lowest g-loads due to its combination of 
  steep 33° banking and wide 320m radius
- Wedge adjustments of ±30 lbs shift rear cross weight by approximately 
  3% — moving the car between loose and tight handling conditions
